This repo is for programs that search for the prime index of the greatest prime factor of products of consecutive integers (the Pidx). Given an integer, m, the most direct way to find the Pidx of m(m+1) is to find the Pidx of m and the Pidx of m+1 and take the greater of the two. Writing in Python, on can use the primefactors(n) routine, in the sympy module, such that Pidx = int(primepi(max(primefactors(m)[-1], primefactors(m+1)[-1]))) which also uses the primepi(n) routine to get the index from the prime number. However when m gets large, doing a complete factorization is very slow, so routines are here to speed up special searches.

The Pidx of m(m+1) jumps around wildly as m increases, expecially when either m or m+1 is a large prime. However, the minim value of that Pidx (min_Pidx) over large intervals tends to jump less, and follow an increasing trend. Whereas the Delta_min program in another repo looks for the minimum value of Pidx-omega for the product over doubling intervals, in this repo the Pidx_search.c program does a fast search to find the min_Pidx with less processing on the omega side, and does it over the intervals between the square roots of primorial numbers.

See the file Pidx_Argument.md for a detailed explanation.
